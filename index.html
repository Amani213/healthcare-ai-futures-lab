⚠ Situation Brief

Eight months ago, your Trust deployed PathwayIQ — an AI-assisted triage and referral prioritisation tool for your Early Intervention in Psychosis (EIP) service. The headline numbers are good: referral-to-treatment times have fallen by 22%, below the 2-week NHS standard for 91% of cases, up from 74%.

Two weeks ago, a newly qualified mental health nurse — Amara Osei — brought you an informal concern. Reviewing her own caseload, she noticed that several young Black men she referred with clear first-episode psychosis indicators were scored as low priority by PathwayIQ, causing delays. She has manually cross-checked 47 cases. Her analysis — informal, not validated — suggests the AI consistently scores this demographic group lower than clinical presentation would predict.

You have spoken informally to the vendor. Their response: "The model was trained on nationally representative NHS data and has been independently validated. Individual case reviews are not statistically meaningful."

Context: Black and minoritised communities are already disproportionately routed to crisis care rather than early intervention in psychosis. The NHS long-term plan explicitly commits to closing this gap. If PathwayIQ is reproducing — or amplifying — historical inequity, the consequences extend far beyond this Trust.
🔴 Emerging Pressure Amara has mentioned the issue to her supervisor. It is now known informally across two clinical teams. If you do not act visibly, she may escalate through formal channels — including the Trust's Freedom to Speak Up Guardian.
